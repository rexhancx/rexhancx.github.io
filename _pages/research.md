## __Current Research__  
---
>All my current research is advised by [Prof. He Meng](http://www.agri.sjtu.edu.cn/En/Data/View/2947) at Shanghai Key Laboratory of Veterinary Biotechnology, SJTU.  
The final goal of my current research is to figure out the function and mechanism of several important genes which severely influence the production traits, apparent traits, or disease resistances of chicken, such as *MSTN*, *TYR*, *DMRT1*, and *HEMGN*. My current research is about chicken genome editing using adenoviral CRISPR-Cas vectors and focusing on *MSTN* and *TYR* genes that relate to muscle development and melanin synthesis respectively. Meanwhile, I developed a low-cost and practical workflow based on amplicon sequencing to analyze CRISPR-induced mutations.  
Till Apr 2021, one SCI paper and one conference abstracts were published, two conference abstracts were accepted, and one patent was applied. Now I'm validating *Tyrosinase* gene function by RNA-seq and working on two SCI journal papers.  


__Project 1__  
__Chicken Leg Muscle *In Vivo* *Myostain* Gene Knockout  through Direct Injection of Adenoviral CRISPR-Cas9 Vector__  
Bachelor dissertation, Nov 2017 - Jun 2018

<img src="/images/ijms.jpg">

* Previous findings about chicken muscle growth and development were not clear.
* To investigate the underlying regulatory mechanisms of chicken muscle growth and development, we performed *in vivo* *MSTN* gene knockout on broiler leg muscle  through direct injection of adenoviral Cas9 vector, and performed *in vivo* fluorescence imaging, sanger sequencing, and RNA-seq to analyze *MSTN* gene pathways.  
* Detected Large-fragment deletion of *MSTN*  in all knockout chicken, and the expression of *MSTN* was downregulated. GO enrichment and IPA analysis revealed many genes that interacted with *MSTN*. Opened up new avenues for gene function and mechanism  studies in avian species.
* One SCI paper and one conference abstracts were published.

__Project 2__  
__A Low-cost and Practical Workflow based on Amplicon Sequencing for Analyzing CRISPR-induced Mutations__  
Aug 2020 - Apr 2021
<img src="/images/seq.png">
<img src="/images/ngs.jpg">

* The high cost and complicated workflow hindered the wide use of NGS methods in CRISPR-induced mutations analysis currently. 
* To analyze CRISPR-induced mutations more cheaply while accurately, we developed a low-cost and practical workflow based on amplicon sequencing.  
* Paired barcoded primers allow multiple samples in one run and reduce the number of primers. NovaSeq 6000 system is up-to-date and efficient which can save both money and time. Our workflow may become a better choice to analyze CRISPR-induced mutations especially for those tight-budget groups. Meanwhile, it can wide the use of both NGS and CRISPR-Cas system. 
* One patent was applied, working on one SCI paper.  


__Project 3__  
__Chicken *Tyrosinase* Gene Knockout through Dorsal Aorta Direct Injection of Adenoviral CRISPR/Cas9 Vector__  
Master dissertation, Jul 2018 - Present

<img src="/images/tyr1.png">

* Previous findings about chicken feather color determination were not clear.
* To investigate the underlying regulatory mechanisms of chicken melanin systhesis and accumulation, we achieved *TYR* gene knockout on black chicken through embryonic dorsal aorta injection of adenoviral Cas9 vector. 
* Detected *TYR* gene knockout both in chicken embryos and living G0 chicken.
* Recently we are screening F1 offspring aiming to generate homozygous F2 and working on one SCI paper

__Project 4__  
__The Mechanism of Vitamin C Protecting Mice Sperm against Cadmium Toxicity__  
Shanghai Key Laboratory of Veterinary Biotechnology, SJTU  
IPP project, Advisor: Prof. Xinhong Li, May 2016 – Jun 2017  
* To culture live mice sperm *in vitro*, sacrificed mice and incubated the epididymis in warm basal medium.
* To investigate how vitamin C (Vc) protect sperm from cadmium (Cd) toxicity, cultured sperm with Cd and a series concentration of Vc, detected the activity of dihydrolipoamide (DLD) dehydrogenase which causes tyrosine phosphorylation of DLD by western blotting and fluorescence in-situ hybridization.
* One SCI paper was published (as co-author).

__Project 5__  
__Construction of Tn5-inserted Mutations in *Tal* Genes of *Xag*__  
State Key  Laboratory  of Microbial  Metabolism, SJTU  
 PRP project, advisor: Prof. Gongyou Chen, Mar 2015 – Mar 2016
